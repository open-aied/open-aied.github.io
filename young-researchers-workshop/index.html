Vision

About This Workshop

This annual series aims to foster sustained, interdisciplinary collaboration among researchers, educators, and technologists exploring the frontiers of educational innovation. Each year, the series spotlights a timely theme at the intersection of pedagogy, technology, and cognitive science, serving as a catalyst for research exchange, joint projects, and scholarly community building.

The workshop provides a platform for young researchers (postdocs, assistant professors, and advanced doctoral students) to present cutting-edge work and form lasting research partnerships.

Research Areas

Workshop Themes

We welcome contributions across a broad range of topics at the intersection of AI and education.

Human-AI Co-Learning

Designing systems where humans and AI develop shared understanding, agency, and adaptive learning strategies.

Learning Analytics & Modeling

Multimodal data analysis, learner modeling, and process-oriented assessment for understanding learning behaviors.

AI Agency in Education

Agentic AI systems, explainability, and the balance between AI autonomy and human control in educational contexts.

Social Implementation

Bridging research and practice — deploying AI-enhanced learning in schools, workplaces, and community settings.

Program

Workshop Structure

A half-day academic workshop designed to maximize exchange and collaboration.

1

Thematic Keynote Presentations

Invited talks by leading researchers on the year's spotlight theme, setting the stage for discussion.

2

Research Paper Presentations & Panels

Young researchers present their latest work, followed by expert panel discussions and audience Q&A.

3

Round Table Discussions & Idea Incubators

Small-group sessions to brainstorm new research directions, identify collaboration opportunities, and incubate joint projects.

4

Collaborative Research Planning

Structured sessions to plan cross-institutional projects and establish ongoing research partnerships.
